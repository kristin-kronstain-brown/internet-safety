## Ages 7–9: "Not Everything Online Is True"

Kids this age start gaming, messaging, searching, and watching videos independently.

Focus on:
- manipulation
- tricks
- scams
- oversharing
- digital permanence

### Main Ideas
1. Introduce "Tricky Behavior"

    - Some people online try to:
        - get attention
        - get information
        - get money
        - get pictures
        - get access to accounts
    
    - Use examples:
        - "Click here to win!"
        - "Don’t tell your parents."
        - "You have to do this now."

    - Teach kids:
        - "Safe adults don’t pressure kids into secrets."

2. Teach Basic Manipulation Tactics

    - Use kid-friendly language.
    - Flattery
    - "You’re so mature."
    - "You’re my favorite."

    Sometimes people say extra nice things to gain trust quickly.
    - Urgency
    - "Hurry!"
    - "You only have one chance!"

    Scammers want people to act before thinking.
    - Secrecy
    - "Keep this between us."
    
    Safe relationships do not depend on hiding things from trusted adults.

3. Explain "Digital Footprints"

    Anything shared online may:
    - be copied
    - be screenshot
    - spread to other people
    - Not as punishment — just reality.
    
    Good phrasing:
    "The internet remembers more than people expect."

4. Basic Password Safety

    Teach:
    - long passwords are stronger
    - don’t reuse passwords
    - don’t share passwords with friends
    - use parent-approved password managers when older
    
    Good rule:
    "Passwords are like toothbrushes: personal and not for sharing."

5. Read before clicking.
