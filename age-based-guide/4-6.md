# Ages 4–6: "My Information Belongs to Me"

## Focus

This is the right age to discuss:
- Privacy
- Asking before clicking or sharing
- Understanding that people online are not automatically who they say they are
- Trusted adults

## Lesson 1: Privacy

Teach that some information is private:
- full name
- age
- birthday
- address
- school
- phone number
- passwords, device codes
- photos
- location
- social media accounts

Simple phrase:
"Private information stays with trusted grown-ups."

Activity: [Word search](../files/private_information_word_search.png)

## Lesson 2: Ask Before You Tap

Teach kids to:
- ask before downloading
- ask before replying
- ask before sharing pictures
- ask before clicking pop-ups

## Lesson 3: Online People Are Real People — But Sometimes Pretending

Keep it simple:
- "Some people online pretend to be someone else."
- "Just like costumes at Halloween, people online can pretend."
- Teach kids how when people hide behind a screen, they might feel more free to be mean or inappropriate.

Activity: Make a list of characters from movies who were pretending to be someone they weren't.

- **Home Alone**: Harry wears a police uniform and acts like he's there to help keep the neighborhood safe. He is really gathering information to help him commit crimes.
    - Lesson: Never share personal information with someone just because they seem friendly or claim to be someone important.
- **Frozen**: Prince Hans pretended to be kind, caring, and in love with Anna.
He was actually trying to become king by tricking everyone.
    Lesson: People don't always tell the truth about themselves.
- **Coco**: Ernesto de la Cruz pretends to be a kind, talented hero. He hides the truth that he stole someone else's songs and betrayed his friend.
    - Lesson: People don't always tell the truth about themselves.
- **Toy Story 3**: Lots-o'-Huggin' Bear (Lotso) welcomes the toys to Sunnyside Daycare and acts friendly. He is actually controlling and treats the other toys unfairly.
    - Lesson: Don't judge someone only by their first impression.
- **Cars**: Chick Hicks acts like he's the best sportsman and wants to win fairly. He cheats and even crashes other racers to get ahead.
    - Lesson: What someone says about themselves doesn't always match what they do.
- **Wreck-It Ralph**: King Candy pretends to be the rightful ruler of the game. He is actually hiding his true identity as Turbo and has taken over someone else's world.
    - Lesson: Some people hide who they really are to fool others.
- **Zootopia**: Assistant Mayor Bellwether acts like a shy, helpful assistant who wants everyone to get along. She is secretly causing problems to gain power.
    - Lesson: Sometimes people pretend to be harmless so others will trust them.
- **Toy Story 2**: Stinky Pete acts like Woody's wise and supportive friend. He secretly plans to keep Woody from returning to Andy.
    - Lesson: Someone may pretend to help you while really looking out only for themselves.
- **Pinocchio**: The Coachman pretends to offer boys an exciting vacation while secretly trapping them.
    - Lesson: If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.

## Lesson 4: Trusted adults

Understanding who a trusted adult is can help kids identify attempts at manipulation later.

Safe Feelings Rule:

- "A trusted adult is someone my family trusts, who helps keep me safe, listens to me, and never asks me to keep unsafe secrets."
- "If something online feels confusing, upsetting, secret, or pushy, tell a grown-up." 


A trusted adult is someone who:
- Keeps you safe and wants what's best for you.
- Listens when you have a question or concern.
- Believes you when something doesn't feel right.
- Helps solve problems instead of making them worse.
- Respects your privacy and doesn't ask you to keep unsafe secrets.
- Encourages you to tell another trusted adult if they can't help.
- Tells the truth.
- Encouraged asking for help.

Activity:  Make a list of characters from movies who proved to be trusted adults.

- Rubeus Hagrid (Harry Potter): Welcomes Harry into the wizarding world. Protects students from danger whenever he can. Treats children with kindness and respect.
    - Trusted adult quality: Caring, dependable, and protective.
- Mufasa (The Lion King): Protects Simba from danger. Teaches important life lessons. Admits mistakes and helps Simba learn from his own.
    - Trusted adult quality: Keeps children safe and helps them make good choices.
- Mrs. Potts (Beauty and the Beast): Comforts and encourages Belle. Looks after Chip and the other castle residents. Stays calm during difficult situations.
    - Trusted adult quality: Kind, patient, and supportive.
- Grandma Tala (Moana): Listens to Moana's dreams and concerns. Encourages her while respecting her choices. Helps her find courage and confidence.
    - Trusted adult quality: Listens, encourages, and offers wise guidance.
- Dory (Finding Nemo): Although forgetful, she stays loyal and helps keep Nemo safe. Works together with Marlin to solve problems.
    - Trusted adult quality: Doesn't give up on helping someone in need.
