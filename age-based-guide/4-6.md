# Ages 4–6: "My Information Belongs to Me"

## Focus

This is the right age to discuss:
- Privacy
- Asking before clicking or sharing
- Understanding that people online are not automatically who they say they are
- Trusted adults

## Privacy
1. Personal Information

Teach that some information is private:
- full name
- age
- birthday
- address
- school
- phone number
- passwords
- photos
- where they are right now
- social media accounts

Simple phrase:
"Private information stays with trusted grown-ups."

## Ask Before You Tap

Teach kids to:
- ask before downloading
- ask before replying
- ask before sharing pictures
- ask before clicking pop-ups

## Online People Are Real People — But Sometimes Pretending

Keep it simple:
- "Some people online pretend to be someone else."
- "Just like costumes at Halloween, people online can pretend."
- Teach kids how when people hide behind a screen, they might feel more free to be mean or inappropriate.

Why does Love is Blind fails? Who you build people up to be is not who they actually are.

## Trusted adults

Safe Feelings Rule:

- "If something online feels confusing, upsetting, secret, or pushy, tell a grown-up." This helps later with manipulation.


## Home Alone references

- Harry pretended to be a trusted adult, a cop.
- Kevin's dad overshared where they were traveling to and how long they would be gone to Harry.
