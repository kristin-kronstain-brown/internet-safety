# A Parent's Guide to Internet Safety

Teach your kids how to live safely in an constantly connected world.

Being real and clear is so important with kids.

## A Simple Family Framework

You can teach almost everything by encouraging your kids to think through these five questions:
- Who is asking?
- Why do they want this?
- How does this make me feel?
- What could happen if I share this?
- Would it be okay if this became public?

That framework scales from kindergarten through adulthood. The rest of this guide uses those questions as a foundation.


## Decide on house rules together

- Participate in regular lessons, just like cyber security at work
- No phones in rooms at night
- No devices behind locked doors. Predators want kids to hide.
- Accept friend requests only from people you know, not friends of friends.
- Always be respectful to others online.
- Tell a parent when you see something that makes you uncomfortable.
- Accountability reviews of kids content
    - Do you know your kids followers? 
    - Read the comments made to your kid
    - Know what's being downloaded
    - App icons can be changed or renamed to help hide them from parents.
    - Get in kids' business without scaring them into hiding things.
